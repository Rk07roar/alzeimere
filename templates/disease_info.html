About Alzheimer's Disease

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects memory, thinking, and behavior. It is the most common cause of dementia, a general term for the decline in cognitive ability severe enough to interfere with daily life.

Symptoms:

  • Memory loss
  • Confusion and disorientation
  • Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  • Changes in mood and personality
